Tracking Notes

  • Weigh daily, track weekly average. If losing >1.5 lbs/week, add 150–200 cal. If weight stalls for 3 weeks, drop 150–200 cal.
  • Hit protein first. If you're short on calories but protein is hit, you're fine. If protein is short, fix it.
  • Keep carbs around training. Don't slash carbs aggressively — they fuel performance and protect mood.
  • Spinach hack. Add a handful of raw spinach to your protein shake — zero taste, strong micronutrient boost. Take a greens powder on days you eat nothing green.

Breakfast

Target: ~450–500 cal, ~40g protein

Protein shake + banana ~420 cal  ·  45g protein
Fastest option. Spinach optional — you won't taste it.
How to make
  1. Add 1 scoop (40g) whey protein + 1 cup milk (or water for fewer cals) to a blender.
  2. Add 1 medium banana. Optional: handful of raw spinach.
  3. Blend 20 seconds. Done.

Solid fallback. Add a protein shake on the side if you need more protein.

Dinner

Target: ~700–800 cal, ~55g protein

Lean ground beef + rice + vegetables ~750 cal  ·  55g protein
Use 90/10 or 93/7 beef — comparable to chicken, more flavor, easier to mix vegetables in. Makes 3–4 servings.
How to make
  1. Heat a pan over medium-high. Add 1 lb 90/10 ground beef. Break it up, season with salt, pepper, garlic powder.
  2. Cook until no pink remains (~7–8 min). Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Add frozen vegetables (broccoli, mixed veg, or peas) directly to the pan. Cover with a lid for 3–4 min to steam.
  4. Serve over cooked white or brown rice. Season with soy sauce, hot sauce, or whatever you like.
  5. Divide into 3–4 containers for the week.
Meal prep chicken + rice + frozen vegetables ~700 cal  ·  55g protein
Cook Sunday, eat Mon–Thu. Makes 4 servings.
How to make
  1. Season 4 chicken thighs or breasts with salt, pepper, garlic, paprika.
  2. Air fryer: 375°F for 20–22 min. Or stovetop: medium heat, 6–7 min per side until cooked through.
  3. Cook 2 cups dry rice (rice cooker or pot per package instructions).
  4. Microwave a bag of frozen vegetables 3–4 min.
  5. Divide chicken, rice, and veggies into 4 containers. Refrigerates well for 4–5 days.
Trader Joe's rotisserie + microwave rice + frozen vegetables ~650 cal  ·  55g protein
Fastest dinner. One rotisserie chicken = 3–4 meals worth of protein.
How to make
  1. Pull chicken off the bone — mix white and dark meat for taste and volume.
  2. Microwave a rice cup (Uncle Ben's or similar) 90 seconds.
  3. Microwave a bag of frozen vegetables 3 min.
  4. Combine. Season with whatever you have — hot sauce, soy sauce, salt and pepper.
Dining hall — grilled protein + carb + vegetable ~650 cal  ·  50g protein
Grilled chicken or fish + rice or potato + one vegetable. Build a plate, don't graze. Use your meal plan for a protein shake or smoothie if you need to top up protein.

Eating Out — Italian

Rule: red sauce > cream sauce. Moderate the pasta portion (restaurants serve ~2× a normal amount). Tomato-based dishes are very manageable on a cut.

Good Chicken parm — solid protein, tomato sauce. Eat half the pasta or push it aside if you're close on calories.
Good Bolognese — meat-based tomato sauce, reasonable. Eat a normal portion of pasta, not the whole plate.
Good Grilled fish / branzino — cleanest option on most menus.
Good Minestrone soup — great starter, low calorie, fills you up before the main.
Limit Alfredo — butter + cream + cheese, highest-calorie option on most Italian menus.
Limit Carbonara — cream and egg heavy. Same category as alfredo.
Limit Bread basket — have 1–2 pieces if you want, but don't mindlessly eat it while waiting.
Tip Enjoy the meal. One Italian dinner a week doesn't break a cut. Pick red sauce, moderate the pasta portion, skip the second bread roll.
